Digital Addiction Risk Assessment System

For Kageshwori–Manohara Municipality

Business Problem

Kageshwori–Manohara Municipality currently lacks a scientific, data-driven model to assess the level of digital addiction risk among students and young individuals. Without a validated predictive model, it is difficult to quantify risk levels, identify vulnerable groups, and support evidence-based awareness or policy planning related to digital well-being.

Project Objectives

  • Develop a machine learning–based digital addiction risk prediction model.
  • Analyze the relationship between digital usage behaviors and addiction risk.
  • Classify individuals into risk categories (Low / Moderate / High).
  • Evaluate and validate model performance using appropriate metrics.
  • Produce a research-ready and policy-supporting analytical output for the municipality.

In Scope

  • Definition of digital addiction–related variables and indicators.
  • Collection and use of survey-based, anonymized data.
  • Data cleaning, preprocessing, and exploratory data analysis (EDA).
  • Feature engineering and selection.
  • Training and testing multiple machine learning models.
  • Model comparison, validation, and performance evaluation.
  • Interpretation of model outputs and key influencing factors.
  • Technical documentation of the final selected model.

Out of Scope

  • Development of web, mobile, or cloud-based applications.
  • Dashboards, visualization portals, or user interfaces.
  • Real-time data collection or monitoring.
  • Clinical diagnosis, treatment, or counseling recommendations.
  • Deployment, maintenance, or operationalization of the model.
  • Integration with external systems or databases.

Assumptions

  • Survey data accurately reflects participants’ digital usage behavior.
  • Adequate sample size is available for model development.
  • Ethical approval and informed consent are obtained.
  • Municipality supports access to educational institutions for data collection.
  • The model will be used for analytical and planning purposes only.

Constraints

  • Limited timeframe for data collection and model development.
  • Dependence on self-reported behavioral data.
  • Data privacy and ethical compliance requirements.
  • Restricted scope limited strictly to modeling and analysis.
  • No budget allocation for system deployment or software products.

High-Level Risks

  • Data bias or inaccuracies due to self-reporting.
  • Insufficient or imbalanced dataset affecting model performance.
  • Overfitting or limited generalizability of the model.
  • Misinterpretation of results without proper contextual explanation.
  • Ethical concerns if results are used beyond intended analytical scope.

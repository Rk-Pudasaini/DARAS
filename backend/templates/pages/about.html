About This Research Project

Data-Driven System for Early Detection of Digital Addiction Risk Among Students

Access the System

Project Overview

The Digital Addiction Risk Assessment System is a research-based platform developed to identify potential digital addiction risks among students. With the rapid increase in smartphone and internet usage, excessive digital engagement has become a major concern affecting students’ mental health, sleep quality, academic performance, and social behavior.

This system leverages data analytics and machine learning techniques to analyze student digital behavior patterns and classify risk levels. The platform provides insights that help educators, schools, and policymakers design early intervention strategies.

Data Collection

Survey-based data collected from students to capture digital usage behaviors, sleep quality indicators, and well-being metrics.

Machine Learning

Predictive models analyze digital behavior variables to identify potential addiction risk levels among students.

Decision Support

The system provides evidence-based insights to support educators and policymakers in promoting healthier digital habits.

Expected Impact

By identifying students at risk of digital addiction early, the system enables schools and local governments to implement targeted awareness programs, counseling support, and responsible technology usage policies.

Ultimately, the goal of this research is to contribute to healthier digital lifestyles and improved student well-being in the digital era.
